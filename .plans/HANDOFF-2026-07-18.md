## Shutdown risks already confirmed

Do not repeat the broad investigation. These are established:

1. Shell-local `requestHardExit()` bypasses controller/container cleanup.
2. Version-lock signal listeners can preempt the main graceful handler.
3. Download cleanup can consume more than the global four-second deadline before terminal restoration and persistence begin.
4. Playback abort can return before the final ledger checkpoint.
5. Debounced config has no shutdown flush.
6. Download workers can claim work after the shutdown running-job snapshot.
7. Background scheduler accepts new work and drains without a bound.
8. Current queue session is left active rather than explicitly recoverable/closed.
9. Container disposal is best-effort but not concurrent-call-safe.
10. Normal, signal, shell-local, and fatal exits do not currently share one lifecycle.
