## What you can do

Kunai is built around a single persistent shell session:

- **Search** TMDB-backed catalogs and anime providers, then inspect results before playing
- **Play** through `mpv` with source, quality, and subtitle pickers when inventory allows
- **Recover** stalled streams (`r`) or **fallback** to the next provider (`f`) when catalogs drift
- **Continue** from local watch history (`--continue`, `/history`)
- **Discover** recommendations, release calendar, and random picks without auto-play
- **Download** for offline viewing (opt-in beta) and play from a local library (`--offline`)
- **Diagnose** with `/diagnostics`, redacted `/export-diagnostics`, and structured `--debug-session` traces

Full capability narrative: [What you can do](/docs/users/what-you-can-do).

## What Kunai is

- A Bun CLI with an Ink shell for search, pickers, history, and playback supervision.
- A local resolver for direct HTTP provider adapters registered in `packages/providers`.
- An `mpv` handoff — Kunai does not replace the player or host video files.
- A diagnostics surface with redacted export paths for support.

## What Kunai is not

<ScopeCallout variant="providers" />

Kunai does not store video on a Kunai server, mirror or redistribute content, guarantee third-party catalog stability, or require accounts for core playback. Browser/Playwright scraping is archive-only — not part of the active beta runtime. Copyright notices belong to the hosting provider, not Kunai.

Details: [Supported and unsupported](/docs/users/supported-and-unsupported).
