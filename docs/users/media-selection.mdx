# Media Selection

Kunai separates provider facts from player state so stream choices can stay available without cluttering the playback view.

## Profiles by mode

Anime, series, and movie playback each has its own media profile:

- Anime uses the anime audio, subtitle, quality, and title-name preferences.
- Series uses the series audio, subtitle, and quality preferences.
- Movies use the movie audio, subtitle, and quality preferences.

These preferences are passed into provider resolution and then into mpv as player hints. Episode numbers remain 1-based in the UI.

## What the pickers mean

- Source picker chooses a provider source or mirror family when the provider reported more than one.
- Stream picker shows the full playable stream inventory.
- Quality picker narrows the stream inventory by quality labels and ranks.
- Subtitle picker switches soft subtitles when they are already known, or asks for provider lookup when needed.
- Audio and hardsub changes prefer cached stream inventory first, then provider lookup only when no matching stream is known.

Kunai only shows these controls when the inventory says they are useful. A title with one stream and one subtitle should not grow a noisy menu.

## Anime vs series/movie behavior

Anime providers often expose sub, dub, hardsub, and source-family differences as different streams. Series/movie providers more often expose quality and subtitle differences. Kunai treats both through the same inventory model:

- Provider inventory stores sources, stream candidates, subtitle candidates, selected stream, and trace evidence.
- mpv state stores what is actually active in the player.
- Diagnostics shows the difference without exposing signed URLs.

This lets Kunai switch a known stream quickly, while still falling back to provider lookup when a language or quality was not discovered yet.
